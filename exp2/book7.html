HARRY POTTER AND THE DEATHLY HALLOWS

Harry, Ron, and Hermione leave Hogwarts to search for and destroy Voldemort’s Horcruxes—the key to his immortality. As the wizarding world falls under Voldemort’s control, the trio goes into hiding, facing danger, betrayal, and emotional struggles. Along the way, they learn about the legend of the Deathly Hallows—three powerful magical objects that may help Harry in his mission. Their journey leads to a final battle at Hogwarts, where friends, teachers, and students unite to fight against Voldemort and his army. Harry discovers that he himself is an unintentional Horcrux and willingly sacrifices himself. However, he survives and ultimately defeats Voldemort once and for all. Many are lost in the battle, but peace is restored. The story ends with a glimpse into the future, showing Harry and his friends grown up, sending their own children to Hogwarts. The book emphasizes sacrifice, love, and the triumph of good over evil.
